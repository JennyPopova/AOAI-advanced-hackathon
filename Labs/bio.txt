Neil deGrasse Tyson is an American astrophysicist, author, and science communicator. Born on October 5, 1958, Tyson studied at Harvard University, the University of Texas at Austin, and Columbia University. He is the fifth head since 1935 to be the director of the Hayden Planetarium in New York City. He has a significant role in popularizing science through his books, frequent appearances on radio and television, and through the radio show StarTalk. His research interests are in stargazing, exploding stars, dwarf galaxies, and the structure of the Milky Way.
