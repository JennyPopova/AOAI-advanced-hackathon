Neil deGrasse Tyson is an American astrophysicist, planetary scientist, author, and science communicator. He was born on October 5, 1958, in Manhattan, New York City. Tyson studied at Harvard University, the University of Texas, and Columbia University.

He served as the director of the Hayden Planetarium in New York City and has written several books for the general public about astronomy and science. Tyson is also the host of the television show "Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey," a reboot of Carl Sagan's "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage."

His research has mainly focused on star formation, exploding stars, dwarf galaxies, and the structure of our Milky Way. His work has brought science to the mainstream, making complex scientific concepts more accessible to the public.
